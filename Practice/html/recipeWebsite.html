Gulam Jamun

Gulam Jamun is a popular Indian dessert made from khoya (reduced milk), sugar, and flavored with cardamom. It is deep-fried and soaked in sugar syrup, making it soft and sweet.

Ingredient:

  • 1 cup khoya (reduced milk)
  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon cardamom powder
  • Oil for deep frying

Preparation:

  1. In a bowl, mix khoya, all-purpose flour, baking soda, and cardamom powder to form a dough.
  2. Divide the dough into small balls.
  3. Heat oil in a pan and deep fry the balls until golden brown.
  4. In another pan, prepare sugar syrup by dissolving sugar in water and boiling it until it reaches a one-string consistency.
  5. Soak the fried balls in the sugar syrup for at least 30 minutes before serving.

Serving:

Serve Gulam Jamun warm or at room temperature, garnished with chopped nuts if desired.

Dodha Barfi

Dodha Barfi is a traditional Indian sweet made from khoya (reduced milk), sugar, and flavored with cardamom. It is cooked until it thickens and then cut into pieces.

Ingredient:

  • 1 cup khoya (reduced milk)
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 1/4 teaspoon cardamom powder
  • 1/4 cup chopped nuts (almonds, cashews)
  • Ghee for greasing

Preparation:

  1. In a pan, heat khoya and sugar together, stirring continuously until the mixture thickens.
  2. Add cardamom powder and chopped nuts, mixing well.
  3. Grease a plate with ghee and pour the mixture onto it, spreading it evenly.
  4. Let it cool and set, then cut into pieces.

Serving:

Serve Dodha Barfi at room temperature, garnished with chopped nuts if desired.

Rasgulla

Rasgulla is a popular Indian dessert made from chhena (fresh cheese), sugar, and flavored with cardamom. It is cooked in sugar syrup, making it soft and sweet.

Ingredient:

  • 1 cup chhena (fresh cheese)
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 1/4 teaspoon cardamom powder
  • 1/4 cup water
  • 1/4 teaspoon lemon juice

Preparation:

  1. In a pan, dissolve sugar in water and bring it to a boil to prepare the sugar syrup.
  2. In another bowl, mix chhena, cardamom powder, and lemon juice to form a smooth dough.
  3. Shape the dough into small balls and gently drop them into the boiling sugar syrup.
  4. Cook until the balls double in size and become soft.
  5. Let them cool in the syrup before serving.

Serving:

Serve Rasgulla chilled or at room temperature, garnished with chopped nuts if desired.
